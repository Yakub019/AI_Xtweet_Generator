The River Learns to Wait

The river learns to wait for spring,
folded beneath a sheet of ice,
its voice reduced to whispering,
a memory it won't think twice.

The mountains do not count the years,
they simply rise and slowly fall,
indifferent to our hopes and fears,
they've always been here, standing tall.

A single seed beneath the ground
holds forests folded in its shell,
it makes no noise, it makes no sound,
yet carries stories it can tell.

The morning light comes soft and slow,
it spills across the waking hills,
it does not ask which way to go,
it simply goes wherever it wills.

A child laughs beside the shore,
collecting shells the tide has left,
each one a small and secret door
to oceans of a world bereft.

The old clock ticks upon the wall,
counting seconds none can keep,
it does not care for great or small,
it ticks through waking and through sleep.

The wind moves through the autumn trees,
and shakes the leaves from tired limbs,
it carries scents upon the breeze
of distant fires and evening hymns.

A lantern glows in someone's hand,
against the dark of falling night,
it does not light the whole of land,
it only holds a little light.

The farmer walks his weathered field,
his boots grown heavy with the clay,
he trusts the soil, trusts the yield,
he plants his hope in disarray.

The city hums beneath the stars,
a thousand windows softly lit,
behind the glass and steel and cars
a thousand lives that quietly sit.

The poet sits with empty page,
and waits for words that will not come,
he wrestles with his private cage,
until the silence turns to hum.

A single star begins to show
above the rooftops, thin and pale,
it does not know the way to go,
it simply drifts along its trail.

The ocean holds a million tides,
each one arriving, each one gone,
it never counts, it never hides,
it simply carries on and on.

A traveler walks a dusty road,
his pack grown light, his feet grown sure,
he carries not a heavy load,
just questions he cannot endure.

The garden wakes at break of day,
the roses lean toward the sun,
they do not ask which is the way,
they simply grow till day is done.

A letter waits inside a drawer,
its ink grown faint with passing time,
it holds a love from days before,
in words that never lost their rhyme.

The mountain stream runs clear and cold,
it winds its way through stone and moss,
it does not know it's growing old,
it only knows the way across.

A candle burns upon the sill,
its flame a small and steady friend,
it does not fear the dark, until
the wax has reached its quiet end.

The harvest moon climbs up the sky,
enormous, orange, full, and slow,
it does not ask the reason why,
it only lights the world below.

A fisherman casts out his line,
into the calm and waiting bay,
he does not rush, he takes his time,
he lets the water have its say.

The library holds silent rows
of stories waiting to be found,
each spine a door that no one knows,
each page a quiet, patient sound.

A child draws upon the wall,
in colors bright and free of rule,
she does not fear to fail or fall,
she only knows the joy of tool.

The winter comes with frost and snow,
it blankets fields in white and grey,
it does not rush, it moves so slow,
it takes its time to have its say.

A bird returns when spring is near,
it builds its nest in branch and eave,
it does not doubt, it does not fear,
it only knows that it should weave.

The bakery opens before dawn,
the smell of bread fills up the street,
the morning stretches like a yawn,
and strangers pass with weary feet.

A grandmother tells an old, old tale,
her voice grown soft with passing years,
she speaks of storms and speaks of sail,
of laughter mixed with quiet tears.

The train moves on along its track,
through fields of gold and rusted red,
it does not turn, it won't look back,
it moves ahead, it moves ahead.

A painter stands before a scene,
his brush held still against the air,
he sees the gold, he sees the green,
he captures what was never there.

The evening settles soft and slow,
the colors fade from red to grey,
the world prepares to let it go,
another quiet, closing day.

A dog waits by the wooden gate,
his eyes upon the empty lane,
he does not know how long to wait,
he only knows he waits again.

The stars come out one by one,
above the rooftops, cold and bright,
they do not care that day is done,
they simply hold the weight of night.

A song drifts out an open door,
it wanders down the empty street,
it does not know what it is for,
it only knows the night is sweet.

The forest holds its ancient hush,
the trees stand tall in silent rows,
they do not speak, they do not rush,
they only breathe the way it goes.
